# ProJobFlo Product Vision

## Product Statement

ProJobFlo helps small contractors move a job from quote to signature to schedule to payment without losing details across texts, notebooks, spreadsheets, and calendars.

## Target Users

Primary users:

- Flooring contractors.
- Tile installers.
- Remodelers.
- Cabinet/countertop contractors.
- Handyman and small trade businesses.

Common user traits:

- Works in the field.
- Needs fast quotes during or shortly after a site visit.
- Often manages customers, crews, payments, and scheduling personally.
- Does not want a complex enterprise CRM.
- Needs simple, practical workflows that survive busy job days.

## Core Promise

ProJobFlo should answer one question:

> What is the next step for this job, and is the money handled?

Everything in the product should support that promise.

## Core Workflow

1. Capture customer and job details.
2. Measure rooms or project scope.
3. Build quote with line items, taxes, deposits, and terms.
4. Send proposal.
5. Customer approves/signs.
6. Contractor schedules the sold job.
7. Contractor tracks deposit, final balance, and completion.
8. Customer and job history remain searchable.

## Product Principles

- Simple beats comprehensive.
- Field usability beats perfect data entry.
- The customer-facing quote should look professional.
- The contractor-facing numbers should be private and practical.
- Every saved item should persist after refresh.
- Payment and schedule data must be trustworthy.
- Cloud sync should be obvious, not mysterious.

## Non-Goals for the Near Term

- Full accounting replacement.
- Enterprise CRM complexity.
- Multi-office workforce management.
- Complex inventory management.
- Full native mobile app before the web app is stable.

## Differentiators

- Built around the contractor job lifecycle, not generic CRM records.
- Combines quoting, signature, scheduling, money tracking, and photos.
- Keeps the workflow direct enough for field use.
- Supports contractor-specific quote templates and preset rates.

## Launch Definition

The product is launchable when a contractor can reliably:

- Log in.
- Create a quote.
- Save the quote to cloud.
- Send or generate customer approval.
- Customer signs.
- Quote becomes sold.
- Contractor schedules the job.
- Contractor records deposit/final payment.
- App refreshes without losing that data.
- Dashboard/customer history/money tracker reflect the same truth.
